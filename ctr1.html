1. Odysseus Method: Remove Distractions

Odysseus, the hero of Homer's epic poem The Odyssey, was able to resist the temptation of the Sirens by having his crew tie him to the mast of his ship. Similarly, you can resist the temptation of social media and other distractions by removing them from your study environment. Turn off your phone, log out of social media, and find a quiet place where you can focus on your work.

2. Pareto Principle: Identify the Important 20%

The Pareto Principle, also known as the 80-20 rule, states that 80% of the results come from 20% of the effort. Identify the most important 20% of your coursework and focus your efforts there. This could mean studying the most important topics, completing the most critical assignments first, or spending the most time on the areas where you need the most improvement.

3. Pomodoro Technique: Use Spaced Repetition

The Pomodoro Technique involves breaking up your study sessions into shorter, focused intervals with regular breaks in between. The basic technique is to work for 25 minutes, then take a 5-minute break. After four cycles, take a longer break. This technique can help you stay focused and retain information by using spaced repetition, which is known to improve memory.

4. Parkinson's Principle: Set Deadlines and Don't Breach Them

Parkinson's Principle states that work expands to fill the time available for its completion. If you don't set deadlines for yourself, your work will likely take longer than it needs to. Set specific deadlines for each task and hold yourself accountable to them. This will help you stay on track and avoid procrastination.

5. Active Recall: Make Notes on Your Own After Understanding the Topic

Active recall involves actively engaging with the material you're studying. Instead of just reading and highlighting your textbooks, try to summarise the information in your own words or make flashcards. This will help you retain the information better and identify areas where you need more practice. By using these five studying strategies, you can take control of your learning, increase your productivity, and achieve your academic goals.
